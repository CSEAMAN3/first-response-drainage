## What Causes a Blocked Toilet?

Toilets are built to handle human waste and toilet paper. Nothing more. But in many cases we attend, other items have been flushed that simply shouldn’t go down. Baby wipes, sanitary products, cotton buds and excess toilet paper are common culprits. Even items like nappies or cleaning cloths can end up causing serious blockages.

Sometimes, it’s not just about what goes down the pan. Over time, everyday use can lead to a buildup of scale or waste inside the pipework. In older systems, we’ve also seen issues caused by tree roots, collapsed pipes or foreign objects that have been sitting undetected for months.

Whatever the cause, the outcome is the same. Water that won’t flush away, slow draining or worse, an overflow. And once that starts, the clock is ticking.

## Spot the Warning Signs Early

Toilet blockages don’t always appear without warning. Often, the system gives you a few early signs that something isn’t quite right.

Look out for the following:

- Water rising higher than usual after flushing
- Water draining slower than normal
- Gurgling noises from the bowl or nearby drains
- Unpleasant smells even when the toilet has not been used
- Incomplete flushes where waste is left behind

If you’re noticing any of these, it’s time to act. Leaving it can lead to a complete blockage or worse, an overflow that damages flooring and creates an unsanitary mess.
