## 1. We Understand Suffolk’s Most Common Drainage Problems

Suffolk is a county of contrasts. There’s its historic towns with Victorian terraced houses, but also sprawling farmland dotted with period cottages and coastal villages facing the North Sea. These varied places bring their own drainage quirks:

- Period homes often have old clay or cast-iron pipes that are more prone to cracks, blockages and issues due to tree roots.
- Rural properties may rely on septic tanks or soakaways instead of mains drainage, which need different maintenance techniques.
- Coastal areas face saltwater corrosion and the risk of sand getting into your pipes, both of which can accelerate damage.

## 6. Prevention Is Better than Cure

One of the biggest advantages of working with our local drainage experts is we can prevent small issues snowballing into big ones. With our regular inspections and maintenance, you’ll never have to worry about serious leaks.

We’ve seen the common seasonal issues that affect homes in Suffolk:

- Autumn. With September on the way, it won’t be long before leaves are clogging gutters and downpipes, leading to an overflow of water. It’s vital they’re removed to prevent this common issue occurring.
- Winter. Frozen pipes in exposed rural properties. This is often one of the worst times for leaky pipes, which is why it’s crucial to have regular maintenance.
- Spring. Tree roots growing during spring can cause cracks and blockages. Pipes which have been under pressure during winter often burst during this time.
- Summer. Dry weather can often cause ground movement that stresses underground pipes.
