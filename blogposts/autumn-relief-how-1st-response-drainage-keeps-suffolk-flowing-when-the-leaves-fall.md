## [What’s Blocking Your Drains This Season?](/drainage-services)

Blocked drains are never random. They build up over time, and autumn tends to bring all the worst offenders out at once. Here are the main reasons customers call us this time of year.

Grease and Fat That Builds and Blocks- It might not seem like much when it goes down the sink, but the fat from your Sunday roast or fry-up does not vanish. It cools, solidifies and clings to the inside of your pipes. Over time it collects more grease and traps anything else passing by, until you’ve got a sticky, solid blockage that refuses to budge.

Pipes That Have Shifted or Broken- Older drainage systems, poor installations or simply natural ground movement can all lead to pipes slipping out of place. Misaligned or collapsed pipes can look like a standard blockage at first, but they require specialist tools and repairs to put right.

Storm Debris That Clogs Outdoor Drains- Leaves, soil, twigs and even litter all get swept into outdoor drains during heavy rain. Once they build up, surface water has nowhere to go. That is when you start seeing flooding on paths, pooling around manholes and water backing up into the property.

Things That Should Never Go Down the Toilet- Wipes, sanitary items, cotton pads, even kitchen roll. We’ve pulled them all out of drains where they do not belong. Unlike toilet paper, these items do not break down. Instead, they get stuck and pile up. Add some grease or silt and you’ve got a full-blown blockage.

Roots That Invade and Wreck Pipes- Autumn is prime time for root growth. Trees send their roots hunting for moisture, and cracked or ageing pipes offer an easy target. Once inside, roots can split pipes wide open, slow water flow or cause total collapse. This is one of the more serious drainage issues we handle, and it often needs more than a simple unblocking.

## Prevention Beats Panic

The truth is, most of the serious drainage problems we deal with in autumn could have been prevented. All it takes is one forgotten outdoor drain full of leaves or one unnoticed pipe fault, and the results can be messy, expensive and disruptive.

We always advise customers to check their drains, gutters and gullies during this season. Keep an eye out for slow-draining sinks or showers. Listen for odd gurgling sounds. If something feels off, it probably is. And the sooner we look, the easier it usually is to fix.
