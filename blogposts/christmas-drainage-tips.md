#### Common Christmas Drain Problems

With more people at home and more food going down the kitchen sink, blockages are one of the most common issues we see in December. Fats, oils and grease are major culprits. When poured down the drain, they can quickly solidify and clog pipes. Even small bits of food that seem harmless can build up over time and cause slow drainage or full blockages.

Bathrooms also see increased use during the holidays. Extra guests mean more pressure on your plumbing. Wet wipes, sanitary products and other non-flushable items often end up in toilets, creating blockages that can quickly escalate into overflows or unpleasant smells.

Cold weather adds another layer of risk. Pipes are more likely to freeze in winter, especially in older properties or outbuildings. A frozen pipe can crack and lead to serious leaks when it thaws, sometimes causing major water damage if not caught early.

#### Simple Prevention Tips

There are a few easy things you can do to reduce the risk of drainage issues this Christmas. In the kitchen, avoid pouring fat or oil down the sink. Let it cool, then scrape it into a bin. Use sink strainers to catch food particles and clean them out regularly.

In the bathroom, place bins in guest toilets so people are less tempted to flush items that don’t belong in the drain. Let guests know what can and can’t go down the toilet if you feel comfortable doing so.

Keep an eye on your drains in the lead-up to Christmas. If you notice slow draining sinks, bad smells or gurgling noises, it could be a sign of a developing issue. Catching problems early can prevent emergencies later.
