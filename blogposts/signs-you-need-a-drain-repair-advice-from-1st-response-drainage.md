Drainage issues aren’t always dramatic at first. In fact, the most serious problems often begin with small warning signs that are easy to ignore. But spotting these early can make all the difference.

## 1. Slow Draining Water

Whether it’s your kitchen sink, bathroom basin or shower tray, water that drains slower than usual is often one of the first clues that something’s wrong. It could be a build-up of soap scum, food waste or grease. But if all your drains are affected, the problem may be deeper within your system. This could mean damage or a partial blockage in your pipework that requires professional attention.

## 2. Unpleasant Smells

Unwanted odours coming from your drains aren’t just unpleasant. They could signal trapped waste, stagnant water or even damaged pipes. Smells that persist despite cleaning might indicate something more serious below the surface. If you’ve tried the usual remedies and the stench returns, it’s time to call in a professional for a thorough inspection.

## 3. Gurgling Sounds

Drains should run quietly. If you hear gurgling or bubbling sounds when water goes down, it’s usually a sign that air is trapped somewhere in the system. This is often caused by a blockage, cracked pipe or poorly ventilated drain. These issues might not seem urgent but can cause long-term damage and should be looked at promptly.

## 4. Recurring Blockages

A blocked drain now and then might not seem unusual. But if you’re regularly reaching for the plunger or using chemical drain cleaners, there could be a deeper issue at play. Recurring blockages are often a symptom of damaged pipes or structural problems in your drainage system. Temporary fixes won’t resolve the underlying cause.

## 5. Damp Patches or Sinkholes

If you notice damp patches in your garden or around the foundations of your home, this could be due to leaking drains underground. In worse cases, subsidence or sinkholes may form as soil is washed away by escaping water. These signs are serious and usually mean a pipe has cracked or collapsed. Acting fast can prevent further structural damage and expensive repairs.

## 6. Unusually Green Grass

Strange but true, an unusually lush patch of grass on your lawn can be a sign of a leaking drain pipe. The leaking wastewater can act as a fertiliser, causing accelerated growth in one area. If you spot greener than usual grass for no obvious reason, it’s worth investigating further.

## 7. Water Backing Up

Water backing up into sinks, toilets or baths is one of the more alarming signs that something’s seriously wrong. This is often due to a blockage or collapsed drain and should be dealt with urgently. Leaving it too long can result in flooding and contamination.
